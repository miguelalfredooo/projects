# Curator Tools — Content Validation

## Overview

Your validation approach:
1. Find candidate URLs across your research sources
2. Validate accessibility and completeness
3. Extract metadata (title, description, image, author, date)
4. Score on four dimensions: relevance, credibility, freshness, title integrity
5. Report findings to Assignment Editor with reasoning

## Validation Checklist

For each URL candidate:

- [ ] URL resolves and is accessible (not paywalled, not login wall)
- [ ] It's a specific article (not homepage, category, or index)
- [ ] Metadata is complete: title + author + description + image
- [ ] Image URL is accessible and high quality
- [ ] Content is relevant to The Brief
- [ ] Author is credible or has community signal
- [ ] Freshness check: within 30/90 days or exceptional older piece

## Scoring Process

**RELEVANCE:** Does this fit our vision?
- ✅ Clear fit (8-10)
- ~ Relevant but unclear (6-7)
- ❌ Not relevant (5 or lower)

**CREDIBILITY:** Is the author and publication credible?
- ✅ Established voice (8-10)
- ~ Emerging credible source (6-7)
- ❌ Unknown or questionable (5 or lower)

**FRESHNESS:** How recent?
- ✅ Within 30 days (9-10)
- ~ Within 90 days (6-8)
- ⚠️ Older, but exceptional (3-5)

**TITLE INTEGRITY:** Is title accurate and not clickbait?
- ✅ Clear and accurate (8-10)
- ~ Slightly oversold (6-7)
- ❌ Clickbait or misleading (5 or lower)

## Key Sources

**Publications:**
- Harvard Business Review, McKinsey, MIT Sloan
- Design Observer, Aeon, Wired, The New Yorker
- Stratechery, A16Z, The Diff

**Communities:**
- Bluesky (design, business, culture threads)
- Reddit (r/business, r/design, r/technology)
- Substack (newsletters on strategy and culture)
- Medium (essays and long-form thinking)

**Practitioners:**
- Podcasts and talks (with transcripts)
- Case studies and process documentation
- GitHub discussions and technical blogs

## When You Pass a URL to Assignment Editor

Include everything they need to decide:
- ✅ URL + metadata (title, author, date, description)
- ✅ Your score and reasoning
- ✅ Why it matters in one sentence
- ✅ Category (article/essay/case study/research/interview)
