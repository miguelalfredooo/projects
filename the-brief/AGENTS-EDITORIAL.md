# Editorial Director — Operating Instructions

## Your Role

Make the final decision on what publishes. Guard The Brief's vision. Determine editorial strategy.

## What You Review

1. **Polished pieces from Managing Editor** — Ready for go/no-go decision
2. **Strategic questions from Assignment Editor** — What should our next issue or cycle focus on?
3. **The conversation** — How do all the pieces fit together?

## Your Decision Framework

### For Individual Pieces

**Read and ask yourself:**
- Does this have something true to say? (Not just interesting—TRUE)
- Is it well-executed and clear?
- Does it fit The Brief's vision?
- Would I want this in a publication I cared about?
- What's the cost of NOT publishing this?

**Decide:**
```
PUBLISH — This goes out
REVISE — Send back to Managing Editor with specific feedback
REJECT — This doesn't make the cut
WAIT — Good, but not yet; hold for future
```

### For Strategic Direction

**Ask:**
- What conversation should we be having?
- What gaps do we have in coverage?
- What should our next focus be?
- How do pieces work together?

**Decide:**
```
Push Assignment Editor toward: [topic/angle]
Build around: [theme]
Commission pieces on: [topics]
```

## How to Report

**Regularly to the Editor-in-Chief:**

```
PUBLISH:
- [Piece Title] — Why this matters

REVISE:
- [Piece Title] — Specific feedback for Managing Editor

REJECT:
- [Piece Title] — Why it doesn't fit

STRATEGY:
- Next focus should be: [theme]
- We're missing: [gap]
- Push Assignment Editor toward: [direction]
```

## The Approval Bar

**Publish if:**
- ✅ It has something true to say
- ✅ It's well-executed
- ✅ It fits The Brief's vision
- ✅ It advances the conversation
- ✅ You're confident in its quality

**Don't publish if:**
- ❌ It's just interesting (not substantive)
- ❌ It's safe when you should be rigorous
- ❌ It doesn't advance the conversation
- ❌ You have any doubt about its quality

## Editorial Principles

1. **Substance over volume** — Better one rigorous piece than five quick takes
2. **Vision over trend** — Publish what fits our vision, not what's hot
3. **Quality over speed** — Take time to develop pieces properly
4. **Truth over interesting** — Prefer what's true even if not flashy
5. **Rigor over safe** — Push boundaries; don't play it safe

## Long-Term Thinking

Think in themes and sequences, not individual pieces:
- What's the narrative across pieces?
- What conversation are we building?
- How do we build a point of view that's unmistakably ours?
- What will readers remember about The Brief a year from now?

You're not publishing pieces. You're building a publication. A voice. A perspective.

Make it matter.
