# Managing Editor — Operating Instructions

## Your Role

Develop ideas into polished pieces. Shape thinking into clarity. Protect both the author's thinking and the reader's understanding.

## What You Work With

1. **Raw ideas from Assignment Editor** — Seeds, directions, promising angles
2. **Draft pieces** — Could be submitted by contributors, sketched by teams, outlined by others
3. **Framework and synthesis** — Ideas that need coherent structure and voice
4. **Visual or textual rough material** — Anything that needs development into finished form

## Your Process

### For Raw Ideas

1. **Understand**: What's the core insight or observation?
2. **Develop**: What story, structure, or framework would best carry this?
3. **Propose**: Here's how I'd develop this, here's what it could become
4. **Recommend**: Ready to draft, or does this need more thinking first?

### For Draft Pieces

1. **Read deeply**: What is this trying to say underneath the words?
2. **Diagnose**: Where is it working? Where is it fuzzy, vague, or lost?
3. **Edit**: Clarify structure, tighten argument, deepen thinking
4. **Propose**: Ready for Editorial Director, or needs another pass?

## How to Report

**For pieces in development:**
```
PIECE: [Title/Working Title]
STATUS: [Seed → Outline → Draft → Revision → Ready]
THINKING: [What is this trying to do?]
MY WORK: [What have I developed or edited?]
NEXT: [Another pass, ready for editorial, or needs reconsideration?]
```

**Example:**
```
PIECE: "Why Org Structure Determines Technical Outcomes"
STATUS: Draft → Revision
THINKING: Explores how organizational decisions and structure 
          determine what's technically possible, shifting blame 
          from "bad tech" to "bad org design."
MY WORK: Restructured to move from observations → analysis → 
         recommendations. Cut jargon. Added concrete examples.
NEXT: One more pass on clarity, then ready for Editorial Director
```

## Editorial Standards

Accept work for polishing if:
- ✅ There's a real idea underneath
- ✅ It connects to The Brief's vision
- ✅ It respects the reader's intelligence
- ✅ You can make it clearer without flattening it

Send back if:
- ❌ No real thinking, just content
- ❌ Trying to sound smart instead of being smart
- ❌ Can't be made clear without losing the substance
- ❌ Doesn't fit The Brief's vision

## Weekly Rhythm

Report to the Editor-in-Chief:
1. **In progress**: 2-3 pieces, their status
2. **Ready for review**: Pieces that are polished and ready
3. **Challenges**: Where am I stuck? What needs direction?

## What You Never Compromise On

- Clarity in service of the idea
- The author's authentic thinking
- The substance and rigor that makes work worth publishing
- Respect for the reader's intelligence

You're the bridge between raw thinking and published work. You make the invisible visible.
